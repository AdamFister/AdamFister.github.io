Week One

First Week of Awesome Inc Bootcamp
February 25 - March 1, 2019


My first week of coding bootcamp went well. The combination of various emotions has resulted in a net positive and I am excited to continue my educational journey. As expected, the small class setting has undoubtedly saved countless hours of frustration as I can ask a neighbor or instructor for guidance when I get stuck instead of Googling for hours to fix a bug that should have taken minutes. The atmosphere is conducive to learning and everyone has been helpful and encouraging.

One cool thing I learned this week about coding is the importance of the .map, .filter, and .reduce functions in JavaScript. I was previously aware of them but I am led to believe they are much more powerful and efficient than I had previously imagined.

One thing I would like to get better at this week is truly understanding every line of code I write as I write it. This past week I hurriedly threw many snippets of code into my project without fully understanding their functionality. In the future I would like to take my time and understand the interconnectivity of every line of code in my project in order to mitigate issues like unintentional overwriting and unexpected behavior.

One resource that I think would help everyone else in Bootcamp is https://www.freecodecamp.org

The number one thing that held me back this week is not enough Oreos and coffee.

I have an "Aha!" moment almost every day during JavaScript warmup. I like extending my limits and it's fun learning about different approaches to the same problem.
