Week One

First Week of Awesome Inc Bootcamp, Feb 25 - Mar 1, 2019

My first week of coding bootcamp went well. The combination of various emotions has resulted a net positive and I am excited to continue my educational journey. As expected, the small class setting has undoubtedly saved countless hours of frustration as I can ask a neighbor or instructor for guidance when I get stuck instead of Googling for hours to fix a bug that should have taken minutes. The atmosphere is conducive to learning and everyone has been helpful and encouraging.

One cool thing I learned this week about coding is the importance of the .map, .filter, and .reduce functions in JavaScript. I was previously aware of them but I am lead to believe they are much more powerful and efficient that I had previously imagined.

One thing I would like to get better at this week is maintaining focus. When making a webpage, I tend to get caught up in the aesthetic aspects which pulls me away from implementing functionality.

One resource that I think would help everyone else in Bootcamp is https://www.freecodecamp.org/

The number one thing that held me back this week is not enough Oreos and coffee. I forgot to have coffee this morning.

I have an "Aha!" moment almost every day during JavaScript warmup. I like extending my limits and it's fun learning of different approaches to the same problem.
